Housekeeping Manager

Hospitality Sector

About the Role

A Housekeeping Manager in the hospitality sector plays a vital role in ensuring a clean, comfortable, and guest-friendly environment. They oversee the entire housekeeping operation, supervising staff, maintaining high cleaning standards, and ensuring guest satisfaction. Housekeeping Managers contribute significantly to the overall guest experience and the reputation of the hospitality establishment.

Key Duties

  • Develop, implement, and monitor housekeeping policies and procedures
  • Recruit, train, schedule, and supervise housekeeping staff
  • Maintain inventory of cleaning supplies and equipment
  • Inspect guest rooms and public areas to ensure cleanliness and adherence to standards
  • Respond to guest requests and complaints related to housekeeping services
  • Coordinate with other departments, such as front desk and maintenance, to ensure smooth operations
  • Prepare and manage housekeeping budgets
  • Order and manage housekeeping supplies and equipment
  • Ensure compliance with health and safety regulations
  • May specialize in a specific area, such as green cleaning or laundry operations (a plus)

Skills Required

  • Strong leadership, communication, and interpersonal skills
  • Excellent organizational and time management skills
  • Proficiency in housekeeping principles and best practices
  • Ability to train and motivate staff
  • Problem-solving and critical thinking skills
  • Attention to detail and a high standard for cleanliness
  • Ability to work independently and as part of a team
  • Experience in the hospitality industry (a plus)
  • Proficiency in housekeeping management software (a plus)

Qualifications and Requirements

  • High school diploma or equivalent (minimum requirement), Associate's degree in Hospitality Management (preferred)
  • Minimum 2-3 years of experience in housekeeping or a related field (e.g., hotel, resort, cruise line)
  • Strong leadership, communication, and interpersonal skills
  • Excellent organizational and time management skills
  • Proficiency in housekeeping principles and best practices
  • Experience supervising housekeeping staff (a plus)
  • Certification in housekeeping management (a plus)

Additional Information

  • Salary: $35,000 - $60,000+ per year, depending on experience, location, and type of hospitality establishment
  • Job Type: Full-time
  • Work Hours: Varied hours, may include evenings, weekends, and holidays
  • Location: Hotels, resorts, motels, bed and breakfasts, and other hospitality establishments
  • Benefits: Health insurance, retirement plans, and other benefits may vary by employer
