Plant Manager

Manufacturing and Production Sector

About the Role

Plant Managers are responsible for overseeing all aspects of a manufacturing plant's operations. They ensure that production processes run efficiently, safely, and on schedule. They manage staff, coordinate with other departments, and implement strategies to improve productivity and quality.

Key Duties

  • Oversee daily operations of the manufacturing plant
  • Ensure production meets quality and quantity standards
  • Manage and supervise plant staff
  • Coordinate with other departments (e.g., logistics, quality control) to optimize plant performance
  • Develop and implement production strategies to improve efficiency
  • Ensure compliance with safety regulations and standards

Skills Required

  • Strong leadership and management skills
  • Excellent organizational and planning abilities
  • Ability to solve problems and make decisions quickly
  • Knowledge of production processes and quality control standards
  • Good communication and interpersonal skills
  • Familiarity with safety regulations and compliance

Qualifications and Requirements

  • Bachelor's degree in Industrial Engineering, Manufacturing, Business Administration, or related field
  • Experience in plant management or a related role
  • Relevant certifications (e.g., Certified Plant Manager, Certified Production and Inventory Management) are a plus
  • Minimum age: 21 years

Additional Information

  • Salary: $80,000 - $120,000 per year, depending on experience and qualifications
  • Job Type: Full-time
  • Work Hours: Typically standard business hours, with flexibility as needed
  • Location: On-site
  • Benefits: Health insurance, retirement plans, professional development opportunities
