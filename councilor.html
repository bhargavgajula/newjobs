Counselor

Education and Training Sector

About the Role

Counselors in the education sector provide guidance and support to students regarding academic, career, and personal development. They work with students to help them achieve their full potential and overcome challenges.

Key Duties

  • Provide academic guidance and support to students.
  • Assist students with career planning and development.
  • Offer personal counseling and support services.
  • Develop and implement educational programs and workshops.
  • Collaborate with teachers, parents, and other stakeholders.

Skills Required

  • Strong interpersonal and communication skills
  • Empathy and active listening skills
  • Knowledge of educational and career development theories
  • Ability to maintain confidentiality and build trust
  • Organizational and time management skills

Qualifications and Requirements

  • Minimum Bachelor's degree in Psychology, Education, or related field (Master's preferred)
  • Relevant certification or licensure as required by local regulations
  • Experience working in educational settings is preferred
  • Minimum age: 25 years
  • Excellent verbal and written communication skills
  • Strong ethical standards and a professional demeanor

Additional Information

  • Salary: $45,000 - $70,000 per year, depending on experience and qualifications
  • Job Type: Full-time
  • Work Hours: Monday to Friday, 9 AM to 5 PM
  • Location: On-site at educational institutions
  • Benefits: Health insurance, retirement plans, professional development opportunities
