Quality Assurance (QA) Tester

IT and Software Sector

About the Role

A Quality Assurance (QA) Tester is responsible for ensuring the quality, functionality, and usability of software applications. They design and execute test plans to identify bugs, defects, and potential issues before the software is released. QA Testers work closely with developers to ensure software meets user requirements and functions as intended.

Key Duties

  • Develop and execute test plans for various software functionalities
  • Identify and report bugs, defects, and usability issues
  • Perform manual and automated testing using various tools and techniques
  • Collaborate with developers to reproduce and resolve bugs
  • Write clear and concise bug reports with detailed steps to reproduce issues
  • Stay up-to-date on the latest testing methodologies and tools

Skills Required

  • Strong analytical and problem-solving skills
  • Excellent attention to detail and accuracy
  • Understanding of software development life cycle (SDLC)
  • Experience with testing methodologies (e.g., black-box testing, white-box testing)
  • Knowledge of testing tools (e.g., Selenium, Cypress)
  • Good communication and collaboration skills

Qualifications and Requirements

  • Bachelor's degree in Computer Science, Information Technology, or related field (preferred)
  • 1-3 years of experience in software testing
  • Certifications in testing methodologies (e.g., ISTQB) a plus

Additional Information

  • Salary: $50,000 - $80,000 per year, depending on experience and qualifications
  • Job Type: Full-time
  • Work Hours: Typically standard business hours
  • Location: On-site or remote, depending on company policies
  • Benefits: Health insurance, paid time off, professional development opportunities
