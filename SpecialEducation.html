Special Education Teacher

Education and Training Sector

About the Role

Special Education Teachers are responsible for educating students with disabilities and special needs. They adapt general education lessons and teach various subjects to students with mild to moderate disabilities. They work closely with parents, administrators, and other professionals to create individualized education plans (IEPs) for students.

Key Duties

  • Develop and implement individualized education plans (IEPs)
  • Adapt lessons to meet the needs of students with disabilities
  • Assess student progress and provide feedback
  • Collaborate with parents, teachers, and specialists
  • Create a supportive and inclusive classroom environment

Skills Required

  • Strong understanding of special education principles and techniques
  • Patience and empathy
  • Excellent communication and interpersonal skills
  • Ability to adapt teaching methods to individual needs
  • Organizational and time management skills

Qualifications and Requirements

  • Bachelor's or Master's degree in Special Education or related field
  • Teaching certification in special education
  • Experience in special education or related roles
  • Minimum age: 21 years

Additional Information

  • Salary: $50,000 - $80,000 per year, depending on experience and qualifications
  • Job Type: Full-time
  • Work Hours: Typically standard school hours, with additional time for preparation and meetings
  • Location: On-site
  • Benefits: Health insurance, retirement plans, professional development opportunities
