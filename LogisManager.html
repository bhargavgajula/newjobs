Logistics Manager

Logistics & Supply Chain Sector

About the Role

A Logistics Manager oversees the entire logistics operation within a company. They are responsible for planning, organizing, and directing the efficient flow of goods and materials from suppliers to customers. This includes tasks like managing inventory, coordinating transportation, optimizing warehouse operations, and ensuring timely deliveries. Logistics Managers play a critical role in minimizing costs, maximizing efficiency, and ensuring customer satisfaction in the supply chain process.

Key Duties

  • Develop and implement logistics strategies to optimize the flow of goods and materials
  • Manage and negotiate transportation contracts with carriers (e.g., trucking companies, airlines)
  • Oversee warehouse operations, including inventory control, storage, and order fulfillment
  • Manage logistics budgets and ensure cost-effectiveness
  • Monitor key performance indicators (KPIs) and identify areas for improvement
  • Develop and maintain positive relationships with suppliers, carriers, and customers
  • Stay up-to-date on industry trends and best practices in logistics
  • May specialize in specific areas such as transportation management, warehousing, or international logistics

Skills Required

  • Strong leadership, communication, and interpersonal skills
  • Excellent analytical and problem-solving skills
  • Proficiency in logistics and supply chain management principles
  • Strong negotiation and budgeting skills
  • Ability to work independently and manage multiple priorities
  • Project management and organizational skills
  • Understanding of transportation regulations and international trade (if applicable)
  • Proficiency in logistics software and technology

Qualifications and Requirements

  • Bachelor's degree in Supply Chain Management, Logistics, Business Administration, or a related field (preferred)
  • Minimum 3-5 years of experience in logistics management or a related field
  • Proven track record of success in optimizing logistics operations
  • Strong leadership, communication, and interpersonal skills
  • Excellent analytical and problem-solving skills
  • CPL (Certified Logistics Professional) certification (a plus)
  • Commercial Driver's License (CDL) (a plus, depending on the specific role)

Additional Information

  • Salary: $60,000 - $100,000+ per year, depending on experience, location, and industry
  • Job Type: Full-time
  • Work Hours: Standard business hours, with potential overtime depending on deadlines or peak seasons
  • Location: Manufacturing companies, warehouses, distribution centers, logistics service providers, and retail companies
  • Benefits: Health insurance, retirement plans, and other benefits may vary by employer
