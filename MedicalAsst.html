Medical Assistant

Healthcare Sector

About the Role

A Medical Assistant provides administrative and clinical support to physicians in hospitals, clinics, and other healthcare settings. They perform a variety of tasks, including scheduling appointments, taking vital signs, assisting with examinations and procedures, and preparing medication. Medical Assistants play a vital role in ensuring the smooth operation of a healthcare practice and contribute to efficient patient care.

Key Duties

  • Schedule appointments, greet patients, and maintain medical records
  • Take vital signs (e.g., blood pressure, temperature, weight)
  • Prepare patients for examinations and procedures by collecting samples, assisting with dressing, and recording medical history
  • Assist physicians during examinations and procedures by sterilizing instruments and handing off equipment
  • Dispense medications and provide basic patient care instructions
  • Perform administrative tasks such as billing, coding, and filing medical records
  • Maintain a clean and sterile work environment
  • Communicate effectively with patients, physicians, and other healthcare staff

Skills Required

  • Strong organizational and time management skills
  • Excellent communication and interpersonal skills
  • Ability to work independently and as part of a team
  • Compassion and empathy for patients
  • Proficiency in computer software used in healthcare settings
  • Detail-oriented and accurate work ethic
  • Ability to follow instructions and protocols meticulously

Qualifications and Requirements

  • Associate's degree in Medical Assisting or a related field (preferred)
  • Diploma in Medical Assisting from an accredited program (may be acceptable in some cases)
  • Certification as a Certified Medical Assistant (CMA) by the American Association of Medical Assistants (AAMA) or a similar organization (a plus)
  • Experience in a healthcare setting (preferred)

Additional Information

  • Salary: $35,000 - $55,000 per year, depending on experience, qualifications, and location
  • Job Type: Full-time, Part-time, or Per diem positions available
  • Work Hours: Varied hours, including evenings, weekends, and holidays may be required
  • Location: Hospitals, clinics, physician offices, and other healthcare settings
  • Benefits: Health insurance, paid time off, and continuing education opportunities (may vary by employer)
