Surveyor

Construction & Real Estate Sector

About the Role

A Surveyor plays a crucial role in construction and real estate projects by accurately measuring and mapping land features. They use specialized equipment and techniques to determine property boundaries, elevations, and topography. Surveyors ensure projects are built on the correct location and comply with legal requirements. Their data is essential for architects, engineers, and construction crews to plan and execute projects successfully.

Key Duties

  • Conduct land surveys to determine property boundaries, elevations, and topography
  • Research historical records and legal documents to verify property ownership
  • Prepare detailed maps and reports of survey findings
  • Set up and operate surveying equipment, such as total stations, GPS receivers, and levels
  • Analyze survey data and ensure accuracy of measurements
  • May specialize in specific areas such as boundary surveys, construction surveys, or topographic surveys
  • Coordinate with clients, engineers, architects, and other stakeholders
  • Obtain necessary permits and approvals for surveying activities (in some cases)

Skills Required

  • Strong analytical and problem-solving skills
  • Excellent attention to detail and accuracy
  • Proficiency in surveying principles and techniques
  • Understanding of mathematics, geometry, and trigonometry
  • Ability to operate and maintain surveying equipment
  • Strong communication and interpersonal skills
  • Proficiency in surveying software (a plus)
  • Knowledge of construction practices (a plus)

Qualifications and Requirements

  • Bachelor's degree in Surveying, Geomatics, or a related field (preferred)
  • Experience in land surveying (an asset)
  • Surveying license (required in most states)
  • Strong analytical and problem-solving skills
  • Excellent attention to detail and accuracy
  • Proficiency in surveying principles and techniques
  • Ability to work independently and as part of a team
  • Good physical condition, as the job may involve walking long distances and working outdoors in various weather conditions

Additional Information

  • Job Type: Full-time
  • Work Hours: Varied hours, may include fieldwork and office work
  • Location: Surveying firms, construction companies, government agencies, and private practices
  • Benefits: Health insurance, retirement plans, and other benefits may vary by employer
