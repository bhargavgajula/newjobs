Construction Manager

Construction & Real Estate Sector

About the Role

A Construction Manager oversees the entire construction process, from planning and budgeting to project completion. They are responsible for ensuring the project is built according to plans, meets quality standards, stays within budget, and adheres to safety regulations. Construction Managers play a critical role in coordinating subcontractors, managing resources, and ensuring the successful execution of construction projects.

Key Duties

  • Review and interpret construction plans and specifications
  • Develop and manage project schedules and budgets
  • Hire, supervise, and coordinate subcontractors and construction crews
  • Ensure compliance with building codes and safety regulations
  • Monitor project progress and identify and resolve issues
  • Manage and control project costs and budgets
  • Maintain accurate project documentation and reports
  • Conduct quality control inspections and ensure adherence to specifications
  • Communicate effectively with all stakeholders, including architects, engineers, and clients

Skills Required

  • Strong leadership, communication, and interpersonal skills
  • Excellent organizational and time management skills
  • Proficiency in construction management principles and best practices
  • Understanding of construction methods and materials
  • Ability to read and interpret construction plans and specifications
  • Problem-solving and analytical skills
  • Budgeting and financial management skills
  • Proficiency in construction management software (a plus)
  • Experience with Building Information Modeling (BIM) (a plus)

Qualifications and Requirements

  • Bachelor's degree in Construction Management, Civil Engineering, or a related field (preferred)
  • Minimum 3-5 years of experience in construction management or a related field
  • Proven track record of successfully completing construction projects on time and within budget
  • Strong leadership, communication, and interpersonal skills
  • Excellent organizational and time management skills
  • OSHA 30-Hour Construction Safety and Health Certification (a plus)
  • Professional Engineer (PE) license (a plus, depending on location and project complexity)

Additional Information

  • Salary: $70,000 - $150,000+ per year, depending on experience, location, and project complexity
  • Job Type: Full-time
  • Work Hours: Varied hours, may include evenings, weekends, and travel depending on project demands
  • Location: Construction sites, offices, and meetings with clients and subcontractors
  • Benefits: Health insurance, retirement plans, and other benefits may vary by employer
