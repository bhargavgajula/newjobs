Food and Beverage Manager

Hospitality Sector

About the Role

A Food and Beverage (F&B) Manager in the hospitality sector plays a crucial role in the success of a restaurant, bar, or other food service establishment. They oversee all aspects of food and beverage operations, ensuring quality, profitability, and guest satisfaction. F&B Managers manage staff, develop menus, control costs, and ensure compliance with health and safety regulations.

Key Duties

  • Develop and manage menus to meet customer preferences and maximize profitability
  • Oversee food and beverage service, ensuring quality, presentation, and efficiency
  • Recruit, hire, train, and schedule food and beverage staff
  • Develop and manage food and beverage budgets
  • Control food and beverage costs through purchasing, inventory management, and waste reduction
  • Maintain positive relationships with vendors and suppliers
  • Ensure compliance with all health and safety regulations
  • Develop and implement marketing and sales strategies to promote food and beverage offerings
  • Analyze sales data and identify areas for improvement
  • Resolve customer complaints and ensure guest satisfaction

Skills Required

  • Strong leadership, communication, and interpersonal skills
  • Excellent organizational and time management skills
  • Proficiency in food and beverage operations and best practices
  • Strong understanding of food costing and menu pricing
  • Inventory management and purchasing skills
  • Ability to work independently and as part of a team
  • Problem-solving and critical thinking skills
  • Strong customer service orientation
  • Knowledge of hospitality industry trends (a plus)
  • Proficiency in food and beverage management software (a plus)

Qualifications and Requirements

  • Bachelor's degree in Hospitality Management, Culinary Arts, or a related field (preferred)
  • Minimum 3-5 years of experience in food and beverage management
  • Proven track record of success in increasing sales and profitability
  • Strong leadership, communication, and interpersonal skills
  • Excellent operational skills and attention to detail
  • Experience with food costing and menu development (a plus)
  • ServSafe Manager Certification (a plus)

Additional Information

  • Salary: $50,000 - $80,000+ per year, depending on experience, location, and type of establishment
  • Job Type: Full-time
  • Work Hours: Long hours, evenings, weekends, and holidays may be required
  • Location: Hotels, resorts, restaurants, bars, and other food service establishments
  • Benefits: Health insurance, retirement plans, and other benefits may vary by employer
