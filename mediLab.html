Medical Laboratory Technician (MLT)

Healthcare Sector

About the Role

A Medical Laboratory Technician (MLT) plays a critical role in supporting patient diagnosis and treatment by performing a wide range of laboratory tests on blood, tissue, and other body fluids. They operate sophisticated laboratory equipment, analyze test results, and report findings to medical professionals. MLTs contribute significantly to accurate diagnoses and effective patient care.

Key Duties

  • Collect blood, tissue, and other body fluid samples from patients following proper safety protocols
  • Perform a variety of laboratory tests using specialized equipment and techniques (e.g., blood chemistry, microbiology, hematology)
  • Analyze test results for accuracy and identify any abnormalities
  • Maintain and calibrate laboratory equipment to ensure proper functioning
  • Document test procedures and results in medical records
  • Adhere to strict safety protocols for handling biohazardous materials
  • Stay up-to-date on new laboratory procedures and technologies

Skills Required

  • Strong attention to detail and accuracy
  • Ability to follow protocols and procedures meticulously
  • Excellent manual dexterity and hand-eye coordination
  • Problem-solving and critical thinking skills
  • Good communication and interpersonal skills to collaborate with healthcare professionals
  • Ability to work independently and as part of a team
  • Proficiency in computer software used in laboratory settings (e.g., Laboratory Information Systems - LIS)

Qualifications and Requirements

  • Associate's degree in Medical Laboratory Technology (MLT) or a related field
  • Certification as a Medical Laboratory Technician (MLT) by the American Society for Clinical Pathology (ASCP) or a similar organization (required in most states)
  • Experience in a clinical laboratory setting (preferred)
  • Strong understanding of laboratory safety procedures

Additional Information

  • Salary: $45,000 - $70,000 per year, depending on experience and location
  • Job Type: Full-time, Part-time, or On-call positions available
  • Work Hours: Varied shifts, including evenings, weekends, and holidays may be required
  • Location: Hospitals, clinics, reference laboratories, and research institutions
  • Benefits: Health insurance, paid time off, continuing education opportunities
