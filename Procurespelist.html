Procurement Specialist

Logistics & Supply Chain Sector

About the Role

A Procurement Specialist plays a vital role in acquiring goods and services for a company at the best possible cost, quality, and terms. They research and identify potential suppliers, negotiate contracts, manage purchase orders, and ensure on-time deliveries. Procurement Specialists contribute significantly to optimizing costs and maintaining a reliable supply chain for the organization.

Key Duties

  • Research and identify potential suppliers for goods and services
  • Develop and issue Requests for Proposals (RFPs) and Requests for Quotation (RFQs)
  • Evaluate supplier bids and proposals based on cost, quality, delivery time, and other factors
  • Negotiate contracts with suppliers to secure the best possible terms
  • Process purchase orders and manage the procurement process
  • Monitor supplier performance and ensure on-time deliveries
  • Build and maintain positive relationships with suppliers
  • Stay up-to-date on market trends and pricing for goods and services

Skills Required

  • Strong analytical and negotiation skills
  • Excellent communication and interpersonal skills
  • Proficiency in procurement principles and best practices
  • Ability to work independently and as part of a team
  • Project management and organizational skills
  • Understanding of contract law and negotiation tactics
  • Knowledge of supply chain management principles (a plus)
  • Proficiency in procurement software and tools (a plus)

Qualifications and Requirements

  • Bachelor's degree in Business Administration, Supply Chain Management, or a related field (preferred)
  • Experience in procurement, purchasing, or a related role (an asset)
  • Strong analytical and negotiation skills
  • Excellent communication and interpersonal skills
  • Proficiency in Microsoft Office Suite and procurement software (if applicable)
  • Understanding of supply chain management (a plus)
  • Certified Purchasing Manager (C.P.M.) designation (a plus)

Additional Information

  • Salary: $50,000 - $80,000+ per year, depending on experience, location, and industry
  • Job Type: Full-time
  • Work Hours: Standard business hours, with potential overtime depending on deadlines
  • Location: Manufacturing companies, retail companies, government agencies, and other organizations
  • Benefits: Health insurance, retirement plans, and other benefits may vary by employer
